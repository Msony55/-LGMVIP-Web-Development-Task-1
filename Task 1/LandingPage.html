The Three Types of Solar Panels

01

Thin Film

If you are looking for a less expensive option, you might want to look into thin-film.These types of solar panels are the easiest to produce and economies of scale.

Thin Film

  • Reaches ~11% efficiency.
  • Cheapest type of panel.
  • Can save on installation due to weight difference.
  • Extremely new development.
  • Can be made with a variety of materials including silicon - 350 times smaller than other variations.
  • Can appear black or blue.
  • Temperature coefficient of -0.2%/C
02

Polycrystalline

You can quickly distinguish these panels because this type of solar panels has squares, its angles are not cut, and it has a blue, speckled look.This leads to a lower efficiency

Polycrystalline

  • Reaches 15-17% efficiency.
  • Newer development.
  • Fragments of silicon crystals melted together.
  • Appear blue in color.
  • Temperature coefficient of -0.3% / C to-0.5%/C.
  • Can withstand 25mm falling at approximately 50mph.
03

Monocrystalline

Monocrystalline panels have a high power output, occupy less space, and last the longest. Of course, that also means they are the most expensive of the bunch.

Monocrystalline

  • Reaches 20% efficiency.
  • Most expensive type of panel.
  • Made from pure silicon.
  • Appear black in color.
  • Temperature coefficient of -0.3% / C to-0.5%/ C.
  • Can withstand 25mm falling at approximately 50mph.
